# DueForge Pitch Script (Apr 10)

## Goal

Tell a founder story in 3 minutes.

- Slides: about 2 minutes and 15 seconds
- Live demo: about 40 to 50 seconds
- Close: about 10 seconds

## Talk Track At A Glance (3:00)

1. 0:00-0:20: Hook story
2. 0:20-0:45: Why this company exists
3. 0:45-1:05: Problem and user
4. 1:05-1:25: Market (Beachhead, SAM, TAM)
5. 1:25-2:10: Product and painkiller demo
6. 2:10-2:35: Alternatives and why DueForge wins
7. 2:35-2:50: Proof this matters
8. 2:50-3:00: Ask and roadmap

## Slide-By-Slide Content

## Slide 1: The Story Hook (0:00-0:20)

Title:

- "I was not missing ambition. I was missing follow-through."

What to show:

- One short personal moment: deadline missed even though effort was high.
- One sentence on the cost: stress, trust, and momentum loss.

Speaker script:

"A year ago, I kept planning aggressively and still missing high-stakes deadlines. Not because I did not care, but because intent was invisible and execution had no accountability loop. That is the problem DueForge exists to solve."

## Slide 2: Why This Company Exists (0:20-0:45)

Title:

- "Purpose: Turn intent into evidence-backed execution"

What to show:

- Company purpose statement:
    - "DueForge is accountability infrastructure for people with high intent and inconsistent execution."
- Three principles:
    - Visible commitments
    - Proof before done
    - Drift surfaced early

Speaker script:

"DueForge exists to make execution reliable. We are building accountability infrastructure so commitments become visible, progress requires proof, and drift is caught before misses become failures."

## Slide 3: Problem and User (0:45-1:05)

Title:

- "Who hurts, and why this pain matters"

What to show:

- Primary user:
    - Execution-strained builder (solo founder, operator, independent professional)
- Pain:
    - Plans are private
    - Deadlines drift silently
    - Progress is reported, not verified

Speaker script:

"Our core user is a high-agency builder. They do not need another planner. They need a system that forces follow-through. Today, commitments stay private, deadlines drift quietly, and progress is often theater."

## Slide 4: Market (1:05-1:25)

Title:

- "Large market, focused entry"

What to show:

- Beachhead:
    - Solo founders, indie builders, and small startup operators actively using accountability and productivity workflows.
- SAM:
    - Knowledge workers and teams buying productivity/accountability software in your initial geographies.
- TAM:
    - Global knowledge work and performance software market.

Use this structure on slide:

- Beachhead: [your number]
- SAM: [your number]
- TAM: [your number]

Speaker script:

"We are entering through a tight beachhead where pain is urgent and willingness to adopt is high, then expanding into the broader productivity and execution software market. We are not starting broad. We are starting where execution pain is constant and expensive."

Note:

- Replace market numbers with sourced values before pitch day.
- Keep citations in speaker notes only, not on slide body.

## Slide 5: What We Built + Painkiller Demo (1:25-2:10)

Title:

- "Capture -> Commit -> Prove -> Check-in"

What to show before demo starts:

- One simple product diagram with four steps.
- One line: "This is already live and pilot-ready."

Demo script (target 45 seconds):

1. Start on landing page
    - "This is DueForge. It is built for execution, not task clutter."
2. Go to login and enter dashboard
    - "Inside, commitments are visible by default."
3. Quick capture one task
    - "I can capture a commitment in seconds."
4. Commit task and submit proof
    - "Now it is no longer intent, it is accountable work with evidence."
5. Show at-risk or commitment feed
    - "And drift is surfaced before the deadline breaks."

One-line close after demo:

- "That is the painkiller loop: visible commitment, verifiable progress, early recovery."

Demo fallback if internet is unstable:

- Use 3 screenshots in sequence:
    1. Landing and clear CTA
    2. Command center with commitment feed
    3. Proof submitted plus at-risk view
- Narrate exactly the same script over screenshots.

## Slide 6: Alternatives and Why We Are Different (2:10-2:35)

Title:

- "Not another task app"

What to show:

- Alternatives:
    - To-do apps
    - Calendar tools
    - Habit trackers
    - Accountability coaching
- DueForge differentiation:
    - Commitment-first workflow
    - Proof required in loop
    - Drift detection and nudges
    - Built-in accountability surfaces

Speaker script:

"Most tools help you list work. DueForge helps you finish it. Our wedge is commitment plus proof plus drift response in one loop, designed for builders who cannot afford silent slippage."

## Slide 7: Proof and Signal (2:35-2:50)

Title:

- "Why we believe this matters now"

What to show:

- Product state:
    - Production deployment live
    - Core loop validated end-to-end
    - Pilot operations runbook ready
- Outcome targets:
    - 10 to 20 weekly active beta users
    - 60% commitments with proof
    - 50% calendar-connected users applying one plan weekly

Speaker script:

"We are past concept stage. The core loop is built, deployed, and operationally testable. We now have clear activation and retention targets and are entering pilot execution with measurable outcomes."

## Slide 8: Ask and Roadmap (2:50-3:00)

Title:

- "What we need and what we will do next"

What to show:

- Ask:
    - Pilot customer intros
    - Operator/founder design partners
    - Feedback on GTM and pricing model
- 3 to 4 months:
    - Run pilot cohorts
    - Tighten proof and nudge loops
    - Validate retention baseline
- 6 months:
    - Improve team accountability features
    - Launch paid pilot plans
- 12 months:
    - Expand distribution channels and integrations

Speaker script:

"Our immediate ask is design partners and pilot intros. Over the next 3 to 4 months, we will run and learn fast from pilot cohorts. Over 6 and 12 months, we scale from strong retention in a focused segment into a repeatable GTM motion."

## Delivery Notes (How To Sound Fundable)

- Speak in outcomes, not features.
- Use decisive language: "we are building" and "we validated," not "we might."
- Never apologize for being early. Show speed and evidence.
- Keep each slide visually simple: one idea, one chart/visual, one takeaway.

## What To Prepare Before The Pitch

1. Lock market numbers and sources for Beachhead, SAM, and TAM.
2. Prepare 3 demo backup screenshots.
3. Put one metrics footer on proof slide (date of metrics).
4. Rehearse to exactly 2:55 to 3:00.

## Final 10-Second Closer

"DueForge exists because ambition should not die in private planning. We are building the accountability infrastructure that turns intention into evidence-backed execution."
